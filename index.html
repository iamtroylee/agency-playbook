Scripts & outreach templates

Cold calls, DMs, emails, follow-ups, and referral asks. All copyable.

Cold call — lead response angle
🔒 For you
"Hey, this is [your name] — I run a local AI agency. I work with small businesses to automate the stuff that's costing them customers without realizing it. Do you have like 60 seconds?" [They say yes.] "Quick question — when someone finds you online at 9 or 10pm and fills out a form or sends a DM, what happens? Do they hear back right away, or is that more of a next-morning thing?" [Let them answer — they'll almost always admit the lead sits there.] "Yeah — that's the most common thing I hear. Most businesses lose 30–40% of inbound leads just because there's no instant response. The customer already moved on to whoever answered first. What I do is set up an AI system that responds, qualifies, and follows up automatically, 24/7. Takes me about a week to build, then it just runs. I've got a 20-min demo — no commitment, just want to show you what it looks like for a business like yours. What does your week look like?"
Alternate opener — Google reviews angle
"Hey [name], this is [your name] — I help local businesses show up higher on Google. I was looking at [Business Name] this morning and had a quick thought. Do you have 60 seconds? For a business that's been around as long as yours, that's probably costing you visibility — not because you're doing anything wrong, just because the review ask isn't automated yet. I can usually help businesses triple that number in 90 days. Want to see how it works?"
Objection handling — quick responses
"We already have a website." → "Great — this isn't about replacing it. It's about what happens after someone visits. Are you automatically following up with every form submission?" "We're not focused on this right now." → "Fair. Can I send you a 2-minute video of what it looks like? No call needed — just something to have when the timing is right." "How much does it cost?" → "Depends on what you need — I build it to fit. Most clients start around $150–$200/month, usually less than what they're losing from missed leads. Can I show you the math?" "We already have a marketing person." → "No conflict — this is backend automation, not marketing. It actually makes their work more effective. 10 minutes, want to see?" "We're doing fine." → "Totally fair. Last question — are you automatically getting Google reviews from customers right now? That's usually the second gap I find."

Taxes — freelance / sole proprietor

W-2 at Mariners + sole proprietor for the agency. Here's exactly what that means. No LLC needed — same deductions, less paperwork, no $800 CA franchise tax.

Why staying freelance is smarter right now
An LLC does NOT lower your taxes — deductions are identical. What it gives you is liability protection, which matters when you have real assets at risk. Your biggest risk right now is a client complaint over a website — low-stakes. California LLC costs $800/year minimum whether you make money or not. Stay sole proprietor until you're clearing $3k+/month consistently, then revisit with a CPA.

Objections & hard moments

The conversations you'll actually have — and what to say.

When they push back on price
"I totally get it — it feels like a lot upfront. Quick question though: what do you think it's costing you right now when someone reaches out at 10pm and doesn't hear back until the next morning? Usually that's 3–5 leads a week just evaporating. Most clients make back the monthly cost within the first couple of missed leads they would have lost. Want me to show you the math?"
When a client wants to cancel
"Totally understand — before we do anything, can I ask what's feeling off? I want to make sure it's not something I can fix in the next week. If it's budget, I can look at adjusting the tier. If it's not working the way you hoped, I'd love 7 days to show you what a couple of tweaks would do."
💡 Most cancellations are fixable in the first 60 seconds. Don't accept the cancel until you've understood the real reason.
Scope creep — when they keep adding "one more thing"
"Happy to add that — just so we're on the same page, that falls outside the included hour for this month, so it'll be $[75–100]/hr. Should take about [X] hours. Want me to let you know the total before I start?"
When a prospect ghosts after seeming interested
Wait 5 business days. Then: "Hey [name] — following up from our call. Totally understand if the timing isn't right. If you do want to move forward, I can hold a spot this week — I'm only taking on 2 more clients this month. No pressure either way." Wait another 7 days. Final follow-up: "Hey [name] — last note from me. I built a quick mock of what [Business Name] could look like — happy to send it over if you're curious. No strings."
When they ask "can you guarantee results?"
"I can't guarantee specific lead numbers — no one can, and anyone who does is lying. What I can guarantee is that your system will work exactly the way we built it: it will respond to every inquiry within 60 seconds, it will send review requests to every customer, and it will text back every missed call. Whether leads convert depends on your business, your prices, and your market. What I'm building is the infrastructure — you do the rest."
When you under-scoped a project and it's taking longer
"Hey [name] — quick update: I ran into a couple of things on the build that are taking longer than I expected. Good news: it's not going to cost you anything extra. I just wanted to let you know we're about [X] days out from launch instead of [original date]. I'd rather take the extra time and hand you something solid. You'll get an update from me every day from here."
⚠️ Under-promise and over-deliver. Never go silent when a project is running late — that's how you lose a client before they even go live.
When a client calls you about something that's actually a GHL platform issue
"Hey [name] — I see the issue and I'm already looking into it. This looks like it's on GoHighLevel's end, not something in your setup. I'm going to monitor it and reach out to support if it doesn't clear within the hour. I'll text you the moment it's resolved — your system is fine."
💡 Clients don't care whose fault it is. They care that you're on it. Be the person who has it handled before they even finish typing their message.
