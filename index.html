Taxes — freelance / sole proprietor

You're W-2 at Mariners + sole proprietor for the agency. Here's exactly what that means. No LLC needed — same deductions, less paperwork, no $800 CA franchise tax.

Why staying freelance is smarter right now
An LLC does NOT lower your taxes — the deductions are identical either way. What an LLC gives you is liability protection, which matters when you have real assets at risk. Right now your biggest risk is a client complaint over a website — low-stakes. A California LLC costs $800/year minimum whether you make money or not. Stay sole proprietor until you're clearing $3k+/month consistently, then revisit with a CPA.
The two taxes you'll owe on freelance income
Self-employment tax (federal)15.3% of net freelance income. Covers Social Security + Medicare — both halves since you're your own employer.
15.3%
California state income taxStacks on your Mariners salary. Up to 9.3% at your income bracket.
Up to 9.3%
Federal income taxCombined Mariners + agency income. Likely 22–24% bracket.
22–24%

Master start-up checklist

Everything in order. Do this once. The sequence matters — earlier items unlock later ones.

Phase 1 — Name & identity (Day 1)
Pick your agency name from the shortlistTop 5: Trailhead Co., Rhythm & Root, Sage & Signal, Peak & Pine, Cadence Co. Check domain at namecheap.com first.
Buy your .co domain at Namecheap (~$10–$15/yr)Get it the day you decide. Don't wait.
Sign up for Google Workspace Business Starter at $6/moworkspace.google.com — gives you yourname@[yourdomain].co
Connect your domain to Google Workspace (15 min)Google walks you through DNS updates on Namecheap. Up to 24 hrs to propagate.
Set up professional email and Gmail signatureyourname@[agency].co — name, title, phone, website. Goes on everything.
Phase 2 — Phone & communication (Day 1–2)
Set up Google Voice (voice.google.com) — freePick a 714 or 949 area code. Business number for all outreach before you spend a dime.
Set up Calendly free (calendly.com)Connect to Google Calendar. Create "20-Minute Demo Call" event type. Get your booking link.
Set up Loom free (loom.com)Record 90-second demo videos for cold outreach follow-ups.
Phase 3 — Banking & money (Day 2–3)
Open free Relay account (relay.fi)Your business bank. Wells Fargo stays 100% personal.
Create 3 Relay sub-accounts: Income, Tax Reserve, OperatingEach gets its own routing number — free and instant inside Relay.
Set up auto-transfer: 35% to Tax Reserve, InstantMove Money → Auto-transfer rules → Profit First → 35% Tax Reserve → Instant → Save.
Transfer seed money from Wells Fargo to Relay Operating ($500–$1,000)Label "Owner's Contribution" in Wave. Covers first months of stack.
Set up Stripe (stripe.com) — connect Relay Income as payoutProcesses all client card payments.
Set up Zoho Invoice (zoho.com/invoice) — connect StripeCreate your invoice template. Set up recurring auto-charges for retainers.
Set up Wave (waveapps.com) — connect Relay accountTag all software as "Software — Business." Bookkeeping starts now.
Phase 4 — Core tools (Day 3–5)
Sign up for GoHighLevel Starter at $97/mo (14-day free trial)gohighlevel.com — your entire client backend lives here.
Watch GHL onboarding videos (1–2 hours)Learn: sub-accounts, pipelines, automations, AI Studio, mobile app setup.
Build a demo account in GHL ("HB Demo Spa")Full Tier 2 setup. This becomes your demo video and proof of concept. Take screenshots.
Record 2-min Loom of your demo accountWalkthrough of chatbot, review text, and client app. Goes in every DM follow-up.
Set up HelloSign (hellosign.com) — free (3 docs/month)For e-signatures on contracts. Test it by signing the template yourself.
Phase 5 — Legal & contracts (Day 5–7)
Copy contract template from Client Templates tab into Google DocsFill in your name and agency name. Save as master — duplicate for each client.
Copy onboarding form questions into a Google Form19 questions, takes 15 min. Share the link after deposit clears. Test it first.
Phase 6 — Online presence (Week 2)
Create Instagram business account @[agencyname]Post GHL demo screenshots, client wins, tips. Your proof wall.
Create Facebook business pageMany HB owners are more active on Facebook.
Claim Google Business Profile (business.google.com) — freeFill in every field: name, phone, website, hours, services, logo. Critical for credibility.
Update LinkedIn to show agency founder roleMany owners check LinkedIn before replying to a cold email.
Phase 7 — Your website (Alongside Client #1)
Build agency website in GHL using the structure in the Website tabDon't build before your first client — build alongside them so you have real work to show.
Connect domain to GHL websiteGHL → Settings → Domains → Add. Point Namecheap DNS to GHL's servers.
Add chatbot to your own agency websiteYour site's chatbot IS your demo. Prospects interact with it and immediately get it.
Embed Calendly in hero AND bottom CTA of websiteEvery visit should have a frictionless path to booking a call.
Phase 8 — Tax setup (Week 1)
Measure your home office square footageDedicated workspace only. $5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500/yr deduction.
Set quarterly tax reminders in iPhone calendarApr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15 — with a 1-week "prepare" reminder before each.
Book a one-time CPA consultYour dual W-2 + freelance situation deserves one professional review. $150–$300 one-time.
Phase 9 — Laptop purchase (When ready)
Decide config: base M5 (~$1,751) vs M5 Pro for Ableton (~$1,949)See Setup tab for full comparison. Check current pricing on Amazon and B&H before buying.
Buy using Relay business debit cardClean paper trail for Section 179 deduction. Save order confirmation in Google Drive "Tax Deductions" folder.
Log laptop in Wave as "Equipment" same dayDon't wait until tax season.
